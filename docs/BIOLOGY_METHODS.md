# Biology and Computational Biology Methods

## Scientific areas

The v0.6.0 Biology Laboratory groups methods into cellular systems, enzymes, genetics, sequence analysis, proteins, population genetics, ecology, physiology, and validation.

## Sequence methods

- DNA composition and GC content
- Reverse complement using IUPAC complements
- Coding- or template-strand transcription
- Standard-code translation
- Six-frame ORF discovery
- Exact motif matching
- k-mer counting
- Needleman–Wunsch global alignment
- Smith–Waterman local alignment
- Aligned-sequence consensus

Browser alignments are intentionally limited to 600 bases per sequence because dynamic-programming memory and time grow quadratically.

## Molecular and biochemical methods

- Michaelis–Menten initial-rate model
- Hill response
- Simplified inhibition models
- Primer screening
- qPCR relative expression
- Protein additive mass and composition
- Kyte–Doolittle hydropathy
- Simplified charge-balance pI approximation

These are screening and analytical models. They do not replace experimentally calibrated assay design, oligonucleotide thermodynamics, structural modeling, or validated bioinformatics pipelines.

## Population and ecological methods

- Hardy–Weinberg expectations
- One-generation viability selection
- Wright–Fisher drift expectation
- Jukes–Cantor correction
- Shannon and Simpson diversity
- Logistic growth
- Euler-integrated Lotka–Volterra model
- Lincoln–Petersen and Chapman mark–recapture estimates
- Allometric scaling

## Physiology and measurement

- Fick diffusion flux
- Van’t Hoff osmotic pressure
- Nernst equilibrium potential
- Exponential microbial growth
- Cardiac output and cardiac index
- Arterial oxygen content
- Sigmoidal dose response
- Serial dilution
- CFU per milliliter
- Deterministic nonparametric bootstrap interval

## Reproducibility

Saved results retain inputs, method identifier, equation, assumptions, warnings, validation timestamp, numerical output, active project, activity history, and notebook/documentation links.
