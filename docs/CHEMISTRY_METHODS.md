# Chemistry Laboratory Methods and Boundaries

The Chemistry Laboratory provides transparent educational and prototyping calculations. Each project calculation stores its method name, input values, result object, timestamp, and Lab version.

## Included models

- Formula composition and average atomic masses from the bundled element registry.
- Exact atom-conservation balancing for ordinary molecular formulas.
- Limiting-reagent calculations from balanced stoichiometric coefficients and supplied moles.
- Ideal solution-concentration arithmetic.
- Monoprotic strong and weak acid/base models at nominal 25 °C conditions.
- Henderson–Hasselbalch buffer model.
- Strong monoprotic acid/base titration model.
- Ideal Ksp dissolution model from stoichiometric ion coefficients.
- Constant-pressure Gibbs relation and ideal standard equilibrium-constant relation.
- Ideal Nernst relation and Faraday electrolysis calculation.
- Arrhenius and zero-, first-, and second-order integrated rate laws.
- Ordinary least-squares linear calibration.

## Not represented automatically

Activity coefficients, ionic strength, nonideal gases, complex formation, polyprotic systems, mixed solvents, temperature-dependent pKw, electrode junction potentials, side reactions, matrix effects, heteroscedastic calibration, weighted regression, instrument drift, certified uncertainty budgets, and chemical hazards require separate expert treatment.
