# Energy and Engineering Methods

Sustainable Catalyst Lab v0.9.0 adds 119 browser-based methods organized into eleven analytical work areas. The methods are deterministic and run locally in the browser. They preserve inputs, equations, assumptions, outputs, validation states, and project provenance.

## Energy balances

Energy closure, load factor, capacity factor, specific energy intensity, exergy efficiency, CHP efficiency, electric heat rate, mass-specific energy, multi-stage conversion chains, and cogeneration allocation.

## Solar

PV output, annual PV energy, temperature adjustment, solar declination, solar elevation, plane-of-array irradiance, solar-thermal output, collector efficiency, PV string sizing, and capacity sizing from annual demand.

## Wind

Turbine power, Betz maximum, annual energy, wind shear, tip-speed ratio, Weibull mean wind speed, air density, turbine thrust, and single-wake deficit.

## Hydro

Hydropower output, annual energy, pumped-storage energy, pump shaft power, penstock velocity, Darcy head loss, metric specific speed, and water-hammer pressure rise.

## Storage

Battery usable energy, runtime, autonomy sizing, measured round-trip efficiency, state-of-charge update, cycle degradation, flywheel energy, sensible thermal storage, hydrogen energy, compressed-air storage, and power-duration analysis.

## Electric power and grid

DC and three-phase power, transformer ratios, resistive line losses, feeder voltage drop, power-factor correction, real/reactive power, prospective fault current, per-unit conversion, demand and coincidence factors, reserve margin, grid emissions with losses, peak shaving, and annual losses.

## Thermal systems

Conduction, convection, radiation, composite U-value, insulation thickness, LMTD, heat-exchanger duty, heat-exchanger effectiveness, heat-pump COP, refrigeration COP, boiler fuel, heat-engine efficiency, pipe loss, and degree-day energy.

## Fuels and hydrogen

Fuel conversion, fuel requirement, electrolyzer hydrogen output, electrolyzer efficiency, fuel-cell electricity and heat, hydrogen compression, stoichiometric air-fuel demand, biogas energy, methane leakage, and fuel-gas flow energy.

## Emissions

Operational emissions, lifecycle intensity, avoided emissions, carbon intensity, carbon payback, two-output allocation, renewable fraction, and emissions trajectories.

## Techno-economics

Simple payback, discount factors, NPV, IRR, capital recovery, LCOE, LCOS, cost of saved energy, levelized hydrogen, carbon-price breakeven, annualized cost, and price sensitivity.

## Reliability

Series and parallel reliability, steady-state availability, Weibull reliability, exponential failure probability, expected energy not served, exact independent-unit loss-of-load probability, redundant-unit adequacy, N−1 capacity, maintenance availability, capacity credit, and SAIFI/SAIDI/CAIDI.

## Validation

The built-in suite includes reference cases for energy closure, PV output, wind power, hydropower, battery energy, three-phase power, conduction, electrolyzer hydrogen, NPV, LCOE, series reliability, and expected energy not served.

These are screening and analytical methods. Detailed engineering design requires appropriate standards, equipment data, calibrated models, safety analysis, and qualified review.
