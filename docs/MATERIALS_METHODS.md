# Materials Science and Characterization Methods

Sustainable Catalyst Lab v0.7.0 adds 49 browser-based materials methods grouped into mechanical, thermal, electrical, magnetic, optical, crystallographic, phase, corrosion, polymer, composite, and microscopy work areas.

## Mechanical behavior

Engineering stress; true stress and strain; elastic constants; resilience; Mode-I stress intensity; Basquin fatigue; Norton–Arrhenius creep.

## Thermal characterization

Linear expansion; steady conduction; thermal diffusivity; thermal-shock screening; DSC enthalpy integration.

## Electrical and dielectric behavior

Resistivity and conductivity; temperature-dependent resistivity; Hall coefficient and carrier density; dielectric capacitance; intrinsic semiconductor carrier density.

## Magnetic behavior

Curie–Weiss susceptibility; magnetic moment; hysteresis-loop energy loss; estimated permanent-magnet energy product.

## Optical characterization

Normal reflectance; absorption coefficient; Tauc band-gap fit; optical path length.

## Crystallography and XRD

Bragg d-spacing; cubic d-spacing; lattice parameter from indexed peak; Scherrer crystallite size; unit-cell density.

## Phase transformations and diffusion

Binary lever rule; Gibbs phase rule; Arrhenius diffusivity; root-mean-square diffusion length.

## Corrosion

Weight-loss corrosion rate; Faradaic mass loss and penetration; Stern–Geary corrosion-current density.

## Polymers and composites

Degree of polymerization; Fox copolymer glass transition; DSC crystallinity; rule-of-mixtures moduli; Halpin–Tsai modulus; composite density; Maxwell relaxation.

## Microscopy and image-derived measurements

Particle-size statistics; area fraction; mean linear intercept; spatial calibration; Abbe resolution.

## Method boundary

The methods are intended for transparent screening calculations, education, experiment planning, and reproducible project records. Instrument calibration, standards-based specimen preparation, uncertainty analysis, microstructural representativeness, phase-database assessment, and professional interpretation remain necessary.
