# Comparative Economics and Development Systems

The v0.17.0 workspace connects national accounts, productivity, growth theory, convergence, trade, structural transformation, labor markets, poverty, inequality, human development, public finance, international development finance, and resilience scenarios in one auditable calculation environment.

Each analysis preserves method and release identifiers, human-readable formulas, executable expressions, labeled numerical inputs and units, labeled outputs and units, assumptions, screening warnings, validation-suite identifiers, timestamps, audit metadata, and project, notebook, and visualization handoffs.

The methods are transparent comparative tools. They do not automate policy, lending, investment, aid allocation, fiscal decisions, or country rankings.
