# Sustainable Catalyst Lab v0.17.0 — Comparative Economics and Development Systems

Version 0.17.0 adds a first-class Comparative Economics and Development Systems workspace with 48 auditable methods and 22 deterministic browser benchmarks.

## Workspaces

- Comparative macroeconomics and national accounts
- Growth, productivity, and convergence
- Trade and structural transformation
- Labor markets, poverty, and inequality
- Human development and public finance
- Development finance and systems scenarios

## Capabilities

The release covers GDP and national-account identities, real growth, savings and investment, current-account and fiscal balances, labor productivity, total factor productivity, capital deepening, ICOR, Solow steady states, growth accounting, convergence, trade openness, export concentration, revealed comparative advantage, terms of trade, real exchange rates, structural transformation, employment and participation, labor income shares, grouped inequality, poverty gaps, wage disparities, human development, education, life expectancy, health spending, taxation, public debt, debt-service burdens, social protection, ODA, FDI, remittances, infrastructure and SDG financing gaps, inclusive growth, resilience-adjusted development, and multi-shock scenario robustness.

## Formula-visible interface

Every method displays a human-readable economic formula, each executable output expression, labeled inputs and units, labeled outputs and units, assumptions, screening warnings, validation metadata, and audit records.

## Focused shortcode

`[sc_lab_comparative_economics_development_systems]`

The main `[sc_lab_app]` interface also receives the workspace.

## Responsible-use boundary

These methods support transparent screening, teaching, comparative analysis, documentation, and scenario design. They do not replace official national accounts, audited fiscal or debt records, representative labor and household surveys, calibrated macroeconomic and trade models, country-specific institutional analysis, distributional assessment, development evaluation, or qualified economic, financial, statistical, legal, and policy judgment.
