# Sustainable Catalyst Lab v0.16.0 — Circular Economy and Industrial Ecology

Version 0.16.0 adds a first-class Circular Economy and Industrial Ecology workspace with 48 auditable methods and 22 deterministic browser benchmarks.

## Workspaces

- Material flow and resource accounting
- Circular products and business models
- Waste prevention and resource recovery
- Industrial symbiosis and eco-industrial networks
- Lifecycle and footprint accounting
- Circular transition and resilience

## Capabilities

The release covers direct material input, domestic material consumption, material footprints, resource productivity, stock accumulation, circular material use, product-life extension, utilization, repair, reuse, remanufacturing, product-as-a-service models, waste prevention, diversion, recycling, composting, materials recovery facilities, anaerobic digestion, extended producer responsibility, by-product exchange, virgin-material displacement, water and heat cascading, symbiosis economics, network structure, embodied and lifecycle carbon, recycled-content savings, energy payback, water footprints, lifecycle costs, eco-efficiency, import dependence, critical-material recovery, supply risk, circular jobs, net present value, and rebound-adjusted savings.

## Formula-visible interface

Every method displays:

- a named accounting, industrial-ecology, or engineering formula
- the executable expression used for each output
- labeled inputs and units
- labeled outputs and units
- assumptions and screening warnings
- validation and audit metadata

## Focused shortcode

`[sc_lab_circular_economy_industrial_ecology]`

## Responsible-use boundary

These methods support transparent screening, teaching, comparative analysis, documentation, and scenario design. They do not replace audited material-flow accounts, product engineering, facility-specific process models, verified lifecycle assessment, regulatory waste reporting, contractual due diligence, market studies, or qualified industrial, environmental, financial, and policy judgment.
